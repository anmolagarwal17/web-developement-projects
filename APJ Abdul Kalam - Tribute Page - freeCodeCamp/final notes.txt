APJ Abdul Kalam was born into a poor Tamil Muslim family in the pilgrimage town of Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu, on October 15, 1931. His mother, Ashiamma, was a housewife and his father, Jainulabdeen, was an imam of a local mosque and a boat owner. He was the youngest in the family with four elder brothers and a sister.

Timeline:

	1931 - Born

	1954 - Went on to attend Saint Joseph's College, Tiruchirappalli, then affiliated with the University of Madras, from where he graduated in physics in 1954.

	1955 - He moved to Madras in 1955 to study aerospace engineering in Madras Institute of Technology.

	1960 - Graduated and joined the Aeronautical Development Establishment of the Defence Research and Development Organisation (by Press Information Bureau, Government of India) as a scientist after becoming a member of the Defence Research & Development Service (DRDS).

	1963-64 - In 1963 to 1964, he visited NASA's Langley Research Center in Hampton, Virginia; Goddard Space Flight Center in Greenbelt, Maryland; and Wallops Flight Facility.

	1965 - Had first started work on an expandable rocket project independently at DRDO in 1965.

	1969 - Received the government's approval and expanded the programme to include more engineers.He was transferred to the Indian Space Research Organisation (ISRO) where he was the project director of India's first Satellite Launch Vehicle (SLV-III) which successfully deployed the Rohini satellite in near-earth orbit in July 1980;

	1970s - In the 1970s, he also directed two projects, Project Devil and Project Valiant, which sought to develop ballistic missiles from the technology of the successful SLV programme.

	1980s - Got approval for allocating â‚¹ 3.88 billion for the mission, named Integrated Guided Missile Development Programme (IGMDP) and was appointed as the chief executive. Kalam played a major part in developing many missiles under the mission including Agni, an intermediate range ballistic missile and Prithvi, the tactical surface-to-surface missile.

	1970s & 1980s - Made an effort to develop the Polar Satellite Launch Vehicle (PSLV) and SLV-III projects, both of which proved to be successful.

	1992 - Served as the Chief Scientific Adviser to the Prime Minister and Secretary of the Defence Research and Development Organisation from July 1992 to December 1999.

	1998 - Along with cardiologist Soma Raju, he developed a low cost coronary stent, named the "Kalam-Raju Stent".

	2002 - He won the 2002 presidential election with an electoral vote of 922,884, surpassing the 107,366 votes won by Lakshmi Sahgal. His term lasted from 25 July 2002 to 25 July 2007

	2012 - In 2012, the duo(he & Soma Raju) designed a rugged tablet computer for health care in rural areas, which was named the "Kalam-Raju Tablet".
	In the same year he launched a programme for the youth of India called the What Can I Give Movement, with a central theme of defeating corruption.

	2015 - Died

Dr. Kalam had a profound love for children and to honour him, in 2010, the United Nations declared 15 October (his birthday anniversary) World Students' Day.


On 27 July 2015, Dr. Kalam while delivering a lecture at Indian Institute of Management Shillong on "Creating a Livable Planet Earth" he was hit by cardiac arrest. Later in hosital at 7:45 p.m IST, he was confirmed dead.
